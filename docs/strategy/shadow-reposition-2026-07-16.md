# Shadow — reposition after a 4-axis pressure test (2026-07-16)

Before betting the founder's center of gravity on Shadow, a 4-axis deep-research
pass pressure-tested the thesis (forcing function, moat, buyer/wedge, solo GTM).
Verdict: **Shadow survives — but only if it repositions.** All four axes
converge on the *same* narrow thesis, and each kills a different piece of the
current framing.

## The one-line thesis (where all four axes point)

**Independent, third-party, tamper-evident audit of the VENDOR AI agents that
banks and fintechs are already deploying — shipped as an OSS-adoptable verifier
core, sold FIRST to the AI-fintechs who must prove governance to their bank
customers, on the examiner-pull + auditor-independence argument.**

## Three traps to stop walking into

1. **Don't lead with the crypto mandate.** (Axis A) No 2026-2027 regulation
   requires cryptographic tamper-evidence for AI decisions. SR 26-2 (Apr 2026)
   *explicitly* carves GenAI/agentic AI out of scope; EU AI Act credit-scoring
   logging is deferred to **2 Dec 2027**. The Ed25519/Merkle/Rekor bundle is a
   *premature vitamin* with an ~18-month runway — a differentiator to *market*,
   not a mandate anyone is forced to satisfy. Real budgeted demand today is for
   *explainable, replayable, retained* AI decision records (examiner pull +
   GDPR Art. 22/Schufa + DORA framing).
2. **Don't position horizontally as an "AI governance platform."** (Axis B) The
   crypto stack is commoditizing into open standards (Sigstore/C2PA/SCITT) and
   native audit is being absorbed by the platforms themselves (Anthropic Claude
   Console audit log, Bedrock invocation logging + CloudTrail, Snowflake Horizon
   Catalog). Horizontal, Shadow is crushed by Credo AI/ModelOp on one flank and
   platform-native logging on the other. The *only* durable seam is
   **vendor-independent, cross-vendor attestation** — a vendor auditing its own
   agent is auditing its own books; regulators value third-party independence.
3. **Don't sell direct to banks as a solo founder.** (Axis D) 9-18 month cycles,
   a SOC 2 Type II gate that itself takes 6-12 months, "nobody gets fired for
   buying IBM," and one-person-vendor continuity risk. There is **no credible
   example of a solo founder building compliance tooling by starting with direct
   bank sales** — that absence is the answer. You die waiting for the first logo.

## The four findings

- **(A) Forcing function = soft-pull, not must-buy.** No US/EU mandate requires
  the crypto layer now; the council + reason-code + replayable-log layer has
  genuine examiner-driven demand. ~18-month runway (US RFI + EU Dec 2027).
- **(B) Moat = one thin seam.** Independent, cross-vendor tamper-evidence bound
  to a specific regulated workflow (ECOA/Reg B adverse-action), sold as
  third-party *assurance*, not as technology.
- **(C) Buyer + wedge.** Champion = Model Risk Management / Chief Compliance
  Officer; signer = CRO / Chief AI Officer. ACV benchmark $30-90K/yr + $10-20K
  impl (Credo up to $150K). Trigger = examiner pressure from the SR 26-2
  carve-out (examiners probe agentic AI every exam; most banks can't prove
  kill-switch/incident capability — that gap *is* Shadow's evidence bundle).
  **Sharpest wedge: govern the third-party AI agents a bank already bought**
  (unowned pain, examined buyer); #2 sell to the **fintechs selling into banks**
  (SOC 2 provably doesn't answer model-validation; faster cycle; seeds bank
  demand). Banks build policy, *buy* the tamper-evident artifact; mid-tier
  lenders (the ICP) lack MRM depth and buy.
- **(D) Solo GTM = OSS-led + sell-to-fintechs, never direct-to-bank.** Precedent:
  Comp AI (open-source SOC 2, ~$1M ARR in ~4 months, ~6 people), Trivy/Semgrep/
  Vanta — every tiny-team win came from a developer-led/self-serve wedge into an
  *easier* buyer first. Time-to-first-paying ~2-4 months via fintech/dev buyers
  vs 12-24+ months direct-to-bank.

## The repositioned GTM sequence (solo-executable)

1. **OSS-core wedge.** Open-source the genuinely dev-adoptable piece — the
   Ed25519 signing + hash-chain **verifier** — optimized for "runs in CI in 10
   minutes." Shadow already has the substrate (`packages/attest-core`,
   `demos/replay/verify-browser.js`, `examples/verify-in-ci/`, the CLI + MCP
   verifier). Package that AS the wedge, not as a feature buried under a
   "banking council" pitch.
2. **Sell the hosted verification + evidence-report layer to AI-fintechs** who
   must hand governance proof to their own bank/enterprise buyers. First paying
   logos; card-priced, weeks-not-quarters cycle.
3. **Convert 1-2 fintechs into public reference customers**; let the fintech's
   deployment be the trojan horse into banks — the fintech *is* the third-party
   agent vendor the bank needs audited.
4. **Defer SOC 2 Type II + direct bank pursuit** until there's revenue and a
   first hire / co-founder to carry security review.

Shadow's single real asset for this: the **warm academic/bank distribution
channel** (Lora, the Yeshiva/IEEE relationships) — use it as a design-partner /
reference accelerant, not a cold enterprise sale.

## What this updates from the current positioning

- Keep the regulatory facts (SR 26-2, GDPR Art. 22/Schufa, DORA) — they're
  right. But "SR 26-2 Tier 3 companion" is a **weak** wedge: SR 26-2 *excludes*
  agentic AI, so it's a gap no one is mandated to fill. Lead instead with
  **independence + third-party-agent governance + fintech-first GTM**.
- The council and the crypto bundle are **features and credibility proof**, not
  the headline or the moat.

## Honest bottom line

Shadow is a real, small, defensible business *if* narrowed to third-party
agent-audit sold OSS-first to fintechs. It is not a horizontal governance
platform, not a crypto-mandate play, and not a solo direct-to-bank sale. Whether
to commit is Alex's call — but if he does, this is the only evidence-backed path
that a solo founder can actually walk.
