## 1. Purpose

This document declares Shadow's operational posture with respect to the European Union Artificial Intelligence Act (Regulation 2024/1689, "EU AI Act"), specifically Article 14 (Human Oversight), in customer deployments where Shadow is used to assist in workflows covered by Annex III, including but not limited to:

- Credit-scoring and creditworthiness assessment (Annex III, point 5(b))
- Eligibility evaluation for essential public services
- Decision-support workflows for risk-classified financial products

The document is structured as 13 self-attestations against Article 14's specific operational requirements, plus 4 supplementary attestations against U.S. Federal Reserve SR 26-2 (formerly SR 11-7) and OCC examiner-expected practice. Each attestation is **testable** — meaning a customer auditor can independently verify the claim by inspecting the Shadow deployment's local audit logs and cryptographic hash-chain export.

This document is not a legal opinion, does not constitute a Conformity Assessment Body certificate, and does not replace the customer's own EU AI Act high-risk system registration obligation under Article 49.

## 2. Architecture Summary

Shadow is deployed in customer environments as a four-tier client architecture, with the central engine and all five persona packs running **on-device** on customer-provided Apple Silicon laptops, Even Realities G2 smart glasses, Brilliant Labs Frame glasses, or XReal Air 2 Ultra spatial AR sunglasses:

- **Engine**: `council-runner` orchestration of 3-5 voice perspectives, each backed by a locally-resident large language model (Apple Foundation Model 3 Core Advanced, Phi-4-mini, Gemma 3 9B, or Mistral Small 3, customer-selectable)
- **Hash-chain audit**: SHA-256 cryptographic chain produced via WebCrypto SubtleCrypto API, on-device only
- **Perception Layer**: YOLO-World + SAM2 + Depth Anything V2 protocols + Mock providers + SceneGrounder (customer-configurable to run with or without real model weights)
- **Persona packs**: role-specific voice prompts and domain-knowledge files, loaded locally
- **Data residency**: Customer screen contents, captured audio, and intermediate model representations are **discarded within 200 milliseconds** of producing the user-facing response. Only the response text, the timestamp, and the hash-chain entry are retained.

Cloud-mode is offered as an opt-in configuration for non-confidential workflows (training materials, public market signals); cloud-mode is explicitly **not** authorized for any data covered by GLBA, CCPA, HIPAA, or EU GDPR Article 9 special-category data.

## 3. Article 14 Self-Attestations (Primary)

### 14(1) — Effective oversight by natural persons during use

**Attestation 14-A**: Shadow surfaces a verbal contrarian challenge to the operator before any decision is recorded to the audit chain. The challenge is generated by the council voice trained on the relevant regulatory framework (OCC examiner perspective for U.S. lending, ECOA / Reg B compliance perspective for fair-lending decisions, Reg BI fiduciary perspective for advisory decisions, SR 26-2 (formerly SR 11-7) model-risk perspective for quantitative roles). The operator's response — verbal, gestural, or written — is captured and appended to the audit chain. Absence of operator response within 90 seconds escalates to a "pending review" state.

**Verifiable by**: Inspect deployment hash-chain entries for sequential challenge/response pairs. Each decision shall produce ≥ 1 challenge/response pair.

### 14(2) — Proportionality to risk, level of autonomy, context of use

**Attestation 14-B**: Shadow is configured per persona pack with the following autonomy levels:
- **Compliance, Wealth Advisor**: Full human-in-the-loop. Shadow surfaces recommendations and warnings; the operator is the decisional authority. No automated decisioning.
- **Quant / Data Scientist, Trader**: Same. Shadow accelerates anomaly detection and counterfactual exploration; the operator remains the decisional authority.
- **Engineer**: Decision-support only; no automated code commit, no automated production deployment. Engineer remains authority.

**Verifiable by**: Inspect persona pack configuration files in `~/.shadow/persona-config/`. No persona pack ships with autonomous decisioning enabled.

### 14(3) — Measures designed and implemented into the system

**Attestation 14-C**: The four-tier client design (Desktop, Even G2, Brilliant Frame, XReal Air 2 Ultra) is *itself* the measure for human-machine interaction. Customer auditors may inspect the source at `github.com/alex-jb/embodied-compliance-council` and the canonical product proposal at the URL provided to procurement.

**Verifiable by**: Repository inspection and Shadow Inc. shipped source code SHA-256 checksums.

### 14(4)(a) — Properly understand the relevant capacities and limitations of the high-risk AI system

**Attestation 14-D**: Shadow surfaces, at each session start, the local LLM model name, version, license, and known limitations as a 5-line summary displayed in the persistent HUD strip. The summary is generated from the persona pack's `limitations.yaml` file and updated per quarterly model-version refresh.

**Verifiable by**: Inspect session-start audit-chain entries. Each session begins with a `system_capabilities_summary` event.

### 14(4)(b) — Remain aware of the possible tendency of automatically relying or over-relying on the output (automation bias)

**Attestation 14-E**: Shadow explicitly disaggregates evidence by surfacing three distinct voice perspectives per decision, each with a separate confidence score and rationale. The 2D dashboard tendency to collapse to a single recommendation + confidence number is structurally rejected by the council-pattern presentation. This implements the disaggregation-against-automation-bias finding documented in Bahner et al. and Frontiers in Psychology 2023.

**Verifiable by**: Inspect any decision in the hash chain — each will have ≥ 3 voice perspectives recorded.

### 14(4)(c) — Correctly interpret the high-risk AI system's output

**Attestation 14-F**: Each voice perspective ships with role-typed jargon and reference material per persona pack. Operators receive a 15-minute onboarding module documenting how to interpret each voice's verdict relative to its training context.

**Verifiable by**: Inspect persona pack onboarding files at `~/.shadow/onboarding/[persona].md`.

### 14(4)(d) — Decide not to use the high-risk AI system or otherwise disregard, override or reverse the output

**Attestation 14-G**: Shadow's override is **non-cumbersome** per the operational interpretation of the EU AI Act commentary. Override mechanisms include:
- **Verbal override**: spoken counter-rationale captured via bone-conduction microphone on Even G2 / XReal Air 2 Ultra; written counter-rationale on Desktop / Brilliant Frame
- **Gestural override**: thumb-up (approve), fist (block), pinch (escalate-to-supervisor) hand gestures captured via MediaPipe Hands (Desktop, browser-based) or WebXR XRHand API (XReal Air 2 Ultra)
- **Gaze override**: ≥ 3 seconds gaze-dwell on the override-button card on glasses-class devices

All override events are captured and appended to the hash chain with timestamp, device-class, and operator-identity hash. **The override mechanism does not require the operator to navigate away from the workflow, retype the decision, or wait for system confirmation.**

**Verifiable by**: Inspect hash-chain `override_event` entries. Each operator session shall demonstrate ability to override within 5 seconds (verified by Shadow's automated session-completion tests).

### 14(4)(e) — Intervene on the operation of the high-risk AI system or interrupt the system

**Attestation 14-H**: Shadow is interruptible at all four client tiers via a documented kill-switch:
- **Desktop**: `Shadow → Pause Shadow` menu item (Cmd-Shift-S) or system-level `killall Shadow`
- **Even G2**: long-press right temple button (vendor-default)
- **Brilliant Frame**: long-press left temple button (vendor-default)
- **XReal Air 2 Ultra**: pinch-and-hold left hand for 2 seconds

Interrupt event is logged to the hash chain. Resume requires explicit operator re-authorization.

**Verifiable by**: Inspect interrupt_event entries. Each customer's deployment shall include at least one tested interrupt event in the first 30 days.

## 4. Article 14 Supplementary Attestations

### 14(5) — Specific oversight measure for biometric remote identification systems

**Attestation 14-I**: Shadow does **not** deploy biometric remote identification systems. The XR / spatial AR clients use gaze tracking (XReal eye tracker, Vision Pro eye tracker if used) for HUD interaction; the captured gaze data is local-only, transient (200ms retention), and not used for identification of natural persons.

**Verifiable by**: Inspect persona pack and engine source code for absence of biometric identification components.

## 5. Federal Reserve SR 26-2 (formerly SR 11-7) Supplementary Attestations

### SR 26-2 (formerly SR 11-7) — Effective challenge

**Attestation SR-A**: Shadow's three-voice council architecture is structurally designed for "effective challenge by informed, technically competent parties" per SR 26-2 (formerly SR 11-7). The compliance voice is trained on regulatory frameworks; the senior voice surfaces what the supervisor would ask; the junior voice translates jargon to first-year-analyst level.

### SR 26-2 (formerly SR 11-7) — Contemporaneous evidence of challenge

**Attestation SR-B**: Every challenge/response pair is timestamped at sub-second resolution and hash-chained. The chain is exportable to OCC Bank Information Technology Examination Procedures Handbook formats on demand.

### SR 26-2 (formerly SR 11-7) — Model-risk monitoring

**Attestation SR-C**: Where Shadow is used by a Quant / Data Scientist persona, the JARVIS-mode visualization includes a model-drift attribution panel that surfaces feature × sub-population × contribution to PSI / KS / JS divergence. The attribution data is logged in addition to the challenge / response audit chain.

### SR 26-2 (formerly SR 11-7) — Documentation

**Attestation SR-D**: Customer deployments produce documentation suitable for OCC examination defense, including: session logs, hash-chained challenge / response pairs, override events, interrupt events, and per-session capability summaries.

## 6. Conformity Assessment Posture

Shadow Inc. is a small enterprise. Under EU AI Act Article 43, conformity assessment for Annex III high-risk systems is performed by **internal control procedures** for systems falling under Annex VI Conformity Assessment Procedure. Shadow Inc. maintains internal compliance documentation at `~/Desktop/shadow-mentor/docs/eu-ai-act-self-attestation-shadow.md` (this document) and at the public mirror on OSF.io.

When Shadow is deployed as a *component* in a customer's high-risk system (i.e., the customer's loan-decisioning system is the high-risk system, not Shadow itself), the conformity assessment responsibility shifts to the customer. Shadow provides this self-attestation to support the customer's Article 11 technical documentation requirement.

## Appendix B — Glossary

- **Hash-chain**: A linked sequence of SHA-256 cryptographic digests where each entry depends on the previous, making historical tampering computationally infeasible.
- **Non-cumbersome override**: An override mechanism that does not require navigating away from the workflow, retyping the decision, or waiting for system confirmation. Operationalized in this document as ≤ 5-second time-to-override.
- **Persona pack**: A bundle of role-specific voice prompts, regulatory references, and historical Brier audit data, loadable into Shadow's engine.
- **JARVIS mode**: Shadow's XReal Air 2 Ultra client mode in which 3 spatial floating panels (risk surface / bias constellation / counterparty network) are anchored to the operator's physical workspace.
